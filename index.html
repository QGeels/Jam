The viola flower, also known as the pansy or viola tricolor, is a charming and colorful bloom with a distinct "face" on its petals. These petite flowers come in various colors, often featuring shades of purple. Violas are popular in gardens and can also be used in salads for their delicate, slightly sweet flavor. They symbolize sentiments of love and remembrance.

Tulips are vibrant springtime flowers known for their diverse colors and elegant shape. They symbolize love and the arrival of spring, making them popular in gardens and as cut flowers.

Roses are classic flowers, known for their beauty and symbolism. They come in various colors, each conveying different emotions. Popular in gardens and bouquets, they have a sweet fragrance and soft, velvety petals, making them a favorite choice for expressing affection.

The hogweed is a plant known for its large, umbrella-like flowers and rugged appearance. There are two main types: common hogweed and giant hogweed. Giant hogweed is infamous for its sap, which can cause severe burns when in contact with the skin. Common hogweed is less dangerous but also has attractive flowers. Despite the potential risks, some people grow hogweeds in ornamental gardens due to their striking appearance. These plants thrive in various environments and are found throughout Europe. While they are impressive, caution should be exercised when handling hogweeds to minimize potential risks.
